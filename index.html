About Me

I recently earned my Ph.D. in Physics from the University of Texas at San Antonio , where my research centered on computational and theoretical biophysics. My work integrates multiscale modeling, nonlinear electrodynamics, and numerical simulations to investigate electrical oscillations, ionic transport, and signal propagation in cytoskeletal filaments, including microtubules and actin. Prior to beginning my doctoral studies, I served as an Assistant Professor of Physics at the University of Chittagong . I am currently pursuing postdoctoral opportunities in computational biophysics, soft-matter physics, and biophysical modeling, with an emphasis on cytoskeletal dynamics, biomolecular electrodynamics, and scientifically rigorous high-performance computing.
